Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 2

Samsung released it’s new Galaxy Z Fold 2. The Fold had durability issues in the past with many more problems on it’s back. Did Samsung solve all of them? Let’s find out.
The Z Fold 2 has re-designed the hinge mechanism to be snappier and uses a display with ultra-thin glass elements in it which is more resistant to scratches. So it should give you this basic idea: it is more powerful, it is more fluid with a 120 Hz refresh rate with an inside screen size of 7.6 inches and outside 6.1 inches. Cameras may not be the best Samsung has ever produced, but it is satisfactory. Samsung has released an advertisement in 2013 showing a foldable phone - this is pretty much it.

All right, that sounds pretty cool, doesn’t it? But is it a wise model? Let's see.

We have two screens here – one on the front and one on the back, but practically how many can we use at a time? – one. We have two camera sets – one on the back, one on the front. But how many do we use at once? – one. We have seen Huawei's folding phone, which does a better job - it has a single screen, single-camera set up. So, it not only takes excellent rear shots but also takes amazing selfies using the main camera set up. Since no. of screens we use here is less obviously battery consumed here is low as well. So Huawei does a better job at folding phones than Samsung? Not exactly. Huawei comes with just a 60 Hz refresh rate and the main problem being Huawei doesn’t support Google Android. So, no google services, which I think is pretty bad.

Huwaei foldable phone was 11 mm thick whereas Samsung Z2 is 15.5 mm thick. The Z2 has inside screen comes with a 120 Hz refresh rate display whereas outside display comes with just a 60 Hz, like seriously? We can experience a jaw-dropping user experience in 90 or 120 Hz - So, 60 Hz is low and is something you do not want to experience all of a sudden from a 120 Hz display.
Alright, the main factor – price, Samsung's first foldable mobile was launched for 1980 USD(that is ultra-expensive for something so fragile). So, I don't think I have to give you an idea of how expensive the second guy would be.

So is this a bad phone? Definitely not - but it could have been more for the price we pay. These issues may be little, but for something that will stick with us for two or more years, you do not want to regret it.

Now onto the bigger problem, does the world need a foldable phone? – my answer is not yet. Smartphones are getting better even at affordable prices with amazing durability and large displays. No matter how durable a foldable phone is, it is still bulliable. If I wanted a screen that big I would just go for a portable laptop with a good display which would cost me half the price I would have to pay for this not so good display. Foldable phones may be the future, but the company needs to listen to what customer needs and what can be changed.

Do we recommend it? – I don’t think a significant part of society would like the idea of foldable phones but if you belong to the remaining zone - I would say, you better wait for a year because even though this mobile is bulliable, it indeed overcame the problems the previous one posed. So, next-gen would probably listen to what customer wants and modify it in a way that we like.
