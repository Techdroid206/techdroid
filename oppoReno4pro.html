OPPO Reno 4 Pro

Alright, how fast would you expect a mobile to be overtaken by its successor in the same series? I would say a minimum of 8-9 months. But Oppo has done something off track this time. It has brought Reno 4 Pro into the market just after 4 months of Oppo Reno 3 Pro release. The Reno series has been good. But, is Reno 4 Pro really a big upgrade?

Overall Reno 4 Pro has definitely overtaken its cousin, Reno 3, by 40% roughly. Since they have introduced graphite cooling system, the chipset won't take much toll too, keeping temperatures down. The 4 pro looks similar to OnePlus 8 screen with a single punch hole camera placed on the same position with same screen size, but a 90hz display on a curved display, that too over a mid-range mobile is something noticeable, it's good.

Reno Pro 3 comes with a glassy metallic finish at the back which is actually plastic though, even though Reno 3 Pro has same plastic finish they have added some clean matte texture which gives a premium feel with soft grip and also 20gms lighter.

Reno 4 Pro Reno 3 Pro
Chipset SD720G HP95
Charge time 35 min 55 min

Well, coming to the camera, both have quad-cam set up but Reno 4 Pro got few upgrades. Night flare portrait mode, this mode basically brings out the light from the foreground and improves the crispness of the picture. Picture samples with this photo are really amusing, the quality, colour differentiation, night mode, ultra-wide mode, 108MP shots, all these modes definitely got higher upgrades. But wait, there is something I miss though, optical image stabilization, well you heard me right, there is no OIS for this one. Instead, they have compensated it with ultra steady video mode which definitely comes handy and increases frame rate decreasing bumpiness in both rear and front modes. Rear camera can record up to 4K videos.

There is another interesting camera feature they have introduced, AI human colour portrait mode which colours out the human in the frame fading out the remaining frame, which is pretty good. It also takes photos very quickly making you feel that, yeah OIS isn’t required for this guy, its pretty fast. Talking about slow-mo shots in this, isn’t that good in 960 fps? It kind of feels like you have added one frame over other instead of a continuous path.

Here comes my favourite part, haptics and headphone jack, you not only have a headphone jack but also some decent quality headphones inside the box(yeah you heard me right, this is true) which is pretty rare these days, thumbs up for that, but haptics, well, haptics seems to be pretty sluggish this time.

Alright, that is it for Reno Pro 4, so the upgrade is for real? It pretty much is, suggestion – well that’s some tricky part, here’s why - even though OPPO claims itself to be a good mid-range mobile, it may be expensive (around 36 -40k) for which you pretty much get a one plus 7T flagship mobile. You might be wondering why didn’t I actually compare it with OnePlus Nord, well OnePlus Nord is relatively less cheap and has a faster chipset of Qualcomm SD 765. Keeping this in mind OPPO Reno 4 Pro is definitely not an option in the budget. But still, Reno Pro 4 is no less than some premium looking phones out there.
